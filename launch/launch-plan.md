# Launch Plan

## Objective

Get the first 1,000 real users by positioning Cash Runway as a free overdraft-prevention and future-bank-balance tool, not a budgeting app.

## Audience

Primary:

- People living paycheck to paycheck
- People coordinating bills around paydays
- People paying down debt
- People worried about job loss or surprise expenses
- People using balance transfers or car loans

Secondary:

- Personal finance creators
- Debt-free journey communities
- Frugal living groups
- Indie/productivity communities that like useful free tools

## Message

Short:

> See your future bank balance before your bills hit.

Plain:

> Cash Runway is a free, no-signup tool that forecasts your future account balance from paychecks, bills, debt payments, transfers, and one-time expenses.

Contrast:

> Budgeting apps tell you where money went. Cash Runway shows whether you will be okay on a future date.

## Launch Phases

### Phase 1: Foundation, Days 1-2

- Confirm app works on desktop and mobile.
- Pick one canonical URL.
- Capture 4 screenshots:
  - Current balance plus forecast summary
  - Daily event-day table
  - Calendar view
  - Scenario/stress test view
- Record a 20-30 second screen demo.
- Prepare a simple feedback form or email address.

### Phase 2: Warm Feedback, Days 3-5

Post to lower-risk places first:

- Personal LinkedIn or X
- Friends/family who manage bills
- Small finance Discords or groups where you already participate
- Indie Hackers or maker communities

Ask:

> Does this match how you think about cash flow?

Do not ask:

> Please share this everywhere.

### Phase 3: Public Launch, Days 6-10

Channels:

- Show HN
- Indie Hackers
- Product Hunt
- Relevant Reddit communities, only after checking rules
- Personal finance Facebook groups, only after checking rules
- Short-form video platforms

Use unique copy per channel.

### Phase 4: Repeatable Growth, Weeks 2-4

- Publish one helpful article per week:
  - "How to forecast your bank balance before payday"
  - "How to avoid overdrafts with a cash-flow calendar"
  - "How to test job loss scenarios before they happen"
  - "Budgeting vs cash-flow forecasting"
- Turn each article into:
  - 1 short video
  - 1 LinkedIn/X post
  - 1 image carousel
  - 1 community discussion prompt

## First 7-Day Calendar

### Day 1

- Capture screenshots and demo video.
- Post a personal build story on LinkedIn/X.

### Day 2

- Send direct feedback request to 10-20 people.
- Ask for clarity, not praise.

### Day 3

- Post in one maker community.
- Collect bugs, confusion, and feature requests.

### Day 4

- Publish first short video:
  - Hook: "Budgeting apps did not tell me if I would overdraft next Thursday."

### Day 5

- Reach out to 10 small personal finance creators.

### Day 6

- Submit Show HN if the app is stable.

### Day 7

- Publish "what changed from feedback" update.

## Best Channels by Fit

High fit:

- Show HN
- Indie Hackers
- Personal finance newsletters
- Debt payoff creators
- TikTok/Reels/Shorts demos
- Facebook personal finance groups

Medium fit:

- Product Hunt
- LinkedIn
- X
- Startup directories

Low fit:

- Paid ads before conversion is proven
- Generic SaaS directories
- Broad "budgeting app" communities that dislike self-promotion

## Launch Copy Rules

- Mention "free" and "no signup" early.
- Mention "local-only data" when talking to privacy-sensitive users.
- Do not promise financial outcomes.
- Do not say "AI" unless a feature actually uses AI.
- Always disclose builder relationship:
  - "I built..."
  - "I am working on..."
  - "I made this free tool..."
